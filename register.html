Stages

Special Stage -The essential feature of a rally is a special stage. Special stage occurs on closed-to-the-public road or track that varies in length from a few kilometers to several tens of kilometers. Each driver begins after a set interval. A rally consists of about 15-30 special stages. The driver who has the best overall time for all special stages is the winner.


Super Special Stage - Super special stage is more spectator-friendly and thus, spectators can watch the rally event clearly. It is the first special stage of a rally and occurs on a shorter track.


Transport Stage - In transport stage, drivers have to compete on a regular road and needs to obey traffic rules, speed limits and so on. The time for finishing transport stage is not limited and this stage is included to move to another special stage when one is finished.
